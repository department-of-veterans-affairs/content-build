# Submit an Enrollment Certification (VA Form 22-1999)

VA Form 22-1999 is the official form School Certifying Officials (SCOs) use to
report a student's enrollment to VA. Timely and accurate certification allows VA
to calculate and pay the student's Monthly Housing Allowance (MHA), tuition and
fee payments, and book stipend.

**This digital form is for Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill) certifications.**
Support for Chapters 30, 1606, 35, and 32 will be added in a future update.
For those chapters, continue using VA-ONCE or the paper form.

[Start your enrollment certification](#start-form){: .usa-button .usa-button--big}

---

## Who should use this form

You should use this form if **all** of the following are true:

- You are a designated School Certifying Official (SCO) or Certifying Official
  of Record at a VA-approved educational institution.
- You are certifying a student's enrollment under **Chapter 33 (Post-9/11
  GI Bill)**.
- You are submitting an **original**, **amended**, or **termination/reduction**
  certification for the current academic term.
- You have **not already submitted** this certification through VA-ONCE or by
  mailing the paper form. Submitting the same certification twice causes
  overpayments and processing delays.

**Do not use this form if:**

- The student is using Chapter 30, 1606, 35, 32, or 31 benefits. Use VA-ONCE
  or the paper VA Form 22-1999 for those chapters until digital support is
  available.
- You are certifying apprenticeship or on-the-job training (OJT) enrollment.
  Use VA Form 22-1999b instead.
- You are not an authorized SCO for this institution.

---

## What type of certification can I submit?

| Certification Type | When to use it |
|---|---|
| **Original** | First certification for this student for this enrollment period |
| **Amended** | Changes to a previously submitted certification (added or dropped courses, tuition correction, program change) |
| **Termination or Reduction** | Student withdrew, stopped attending, or reduced enrollment — report the last date of attendance |

---

## What you'll need to complete this form

Gather this information before you start. The form will time out after **60
minutes** of inactivity and your progress may not be saved.

**About your institution:**
- VA Facility Code (8-digit number on your VA approval letter)
- Institution name, address, and type (public, private non-profit, or
  proprietary/for-profit)
- Whether your institution has a Yellow Ribbon Agreement with VA (for
  Chapter 33 private-school certifications)

**About the student:**
- Student's Social Security Number (SSN) or VA file number
- Student's full legal name and date of birth
- Student's email address (optional — used to notify the student of
  certification receipt)

**About the enrollment:**
- Training period begin and end dates
- Program of study (name, degree level, and standard length)
- Credit hours or clock hours enrolled this term
- Your institution's full-time credit hour standard for this program level
- Tuition charged and mandatory fees for the term (itemized by fee type)
- Yellow Ribbon contribution amount (if applicable)

**For amended certifications, also gather:**
- Your prior certification reference number
- Effective date of the enrollment change
- Description of what changed (courses added/dropped, hours, tuition)

**For termination or reduction certifications, also gather:**
- Reason for termination or reduction
- Student's last date of attendance
- Official withdrawal date (if different from last date of attendance)
- Whether any non-punitive grades (W, I, NC) were received this term

---

## How long this takes

Most SCOs complete this form in **15 to 30 minutes** for a straightforward
original certification. Amended and termination certifications may take
slightly longer depending on the complexity of the change.

---

## Advance certifications

You can submit a certification up to **120 days before** the term start date.
VA will hold the certification and begin processing it when the term is
closer to starting. The form will show you a notice if your term start date
triggers the advance certification window.

---

## About VA-ONCE

This digital form works alongside — but does not replace — VA-ONCE. If you
already use VA-ONCE for most of your certifications, you can continue to do
so. Use this VA.gov form when:

- You are at a small institution and have not registered for VA-ONCE.
- You are submitting a correction or amended certification outside your
  normal VA-ONCE workflow.
- You need to submit a retroactive or non-standard certification.

**Do not submit the same certification in both places.** Duplicate
certifications cause overpayments and can create a debt for the student.

## Privacy Act Statement

The information you provide is covered under the Privacy Act of 1974 (5 U.S.C.
§ 552a). VA will use this information to process enrollment certifications for
GI Bill education benefits under 38 U.S.C. Chapters 30, 33, 35, and 1606.
Providing the information is voluntary, but failure to furnish the requested
information may delay or prevent processing of the certification. The
information may be disclosed as permitted by law, including to other federal
agencies for program oversight and to educational institutions for
benefit-related purposes.
