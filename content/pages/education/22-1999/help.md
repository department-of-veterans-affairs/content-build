# Help with VA Form 22-1999 (Enrollment Certification)

Get answers to common questions about submitting enrollment certifications
online using VA Form 22-1999. If you need more help, contact us using the
information at the bottom of this page.

---

## Frequently asked questions

### General questions

#### Is this the right form for me?

Use this form if you are a designated School Certifying Official (SCO) at a
VA-approved educational institution and you need to submit an **original**,
**amended**, or **termination/reduction** enrollment certification for a
student using **Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill)** benefits.

If you are certifying a student under **Chapter 30** (MGIB–Active Duty),
**Chapter 1606** (MGIB–Selected Reserve), **Chapter 35** (DEA), or
**Chapter 31** (VR&E), use VA-ONCE or submit the paper VA Form 22-1999 to
your Regional Processing Office. Digital support for those chapters will be
added in a future update.

If you are certifying **apprenticeship or on-the-job training (OJT)**
enrollment, use **VA Form 22-1999b** instead.

#### What if I already use VA-ONCE?

You can keep using VA-ONCE for your regular certifications. This VA.gov form
is especially useful if you are at a smaller institution that has not
registered for VA-ONCE, or if you need to submit a correction or amended
certification outside your normal VA-ONCE workflow.

**Important:** Do not submit the same certification in both VA-ONCE and this
digital form. Submitting the same certification twice can cause an overpayment
and may create a debt that the student will have to repay.

#### Can I submit a paper form instead of using this digital form?

Yes. The paper VA Form 22-1999 is still accepted. Mail completed paper forms
to the VA Regional Processing Office that serves your state. Be aware that
paper certifications can take 7 to 14 days to arrive and additional weeks to
process, which may delay the student's housing allowance and other payments.
The digital form reaches VA the same day you submit it.

---

### Using the digital form

#### Do I need to create an account to use this form?

Yes. You must sign in with a verified Login.gov or ID.me account to submit
certifications digitally. VA uses authentication to verify your identity and
confirm that you are an authorized SCO for your institution before accepting
a submission.

If you do not have a Login.gov or ID.me account, you can create one for free.
Go to [VA.gov sign-in page](/sign-in/) to get started.

#### How do I find my VA Facility Code?

Your VA Facility Code is an 8-digit number assigned to your institution when
VA approved it for GI Bill purposes. You can find your facility code:

- On your institution's VA approval letter from your State Approving Agency
  (SAA)
- By contacting your State Approving Agency (SAA)
- By searching the [GI Bill School Locator](https://www.benefits.va.gov/gibill/gi_bill_comparison_tool.asp)

If the facility code you enter is not recognized, the form will show you an
error message. Verify the code and try again. If the problem continues,
contact your Regional Processing Office.

#### What is an advance certification?

An advance certification is a certification you submit before the enrollment
period begins — up to **120 days before the term start date**. VA will receive
and hold the certification, then begin processing it closer to the term start
date. If you submit an advance certification, the form will show you a notice
explaining when VA will begin processing it.

#### What is the difference between an amended certification and an original certification?

An **original certification** is the first certification you submit for a
student for a given enrollment period.

An **amended certification** is a correction or update to a certification you
have already submitted. Submit an amended certification if the student added
or dropped courses, if the tuition or fees changed, if the student's
training time classification changed, or if you need to correct any other
information in the original certification.

#### What happens after I submit?

After you submit, you will see a confirmation page with a **reference number**.
Save or print that page for your records. VA will review your certification
and begin processing it. The target processing time for digital certifications
is **30 days or fewer**.

If you submitted an original certification and the student's enrollment
changes, you can return to this form to submit an amended certification.

### Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill) specific questions

#### How is training time calculated?

Training time is based on the number of credit or clock hours a student is
enrolled in, divided by your institution's full-time standard for that
program level.

For example, if your institution defines full-time as 12 credit hours for
undergraduate students and the student is enrolled in 9 credit hours, the
rate of pursuit is 75% (9 ÷ 12), which classifies as **three-quarter time**.

The form calculates the rate of pursuit automatically based on the hours you
enter and the full-time standard you provide. You must confirm the training
time classification before continuing.

- Full-time: 100% of the full-time standard
- Three-quarter time: 75% to less than 100%
- Half-time: 50% to less than 75%
- Less than half-time: less than 50%

**Important:** If a student's rate of pursuit is less than 50%, VA will not
pay a Monthly Housing Allowance (MHA) for Chapter 33.

#### Does active duty affect the Monthly Housing Allowance?

Yes. If a student is currently on active duty, VA pays **$0 MHA** for
Chapter 33, regardless of enrollment status. The form will ask about active
duty status and will show a notice if this applies.

#### How do tuition caps work?

For **public institutions**, VA pays the **in-state tuition rate** for the
state where the school is located, even if the student is paying out-of-state
tuition. The form will display the current in-state cap for your state if
this applies.

For **private and proprietary institutions**, VA pays up to the **national
maximum** ($27,120.05 for academic year 2023–24). If the tuition you enter
exceeds the national maximum, the form will show a warning that payment will
be capped.

#### What is the Yellow Ribbon Program?

The Yellow Ribbon Program allows VA and participating private institutions to
share the cost of tuition that exceeds the national maximum for Chapter 33.
If your institution has a Yellow Ribbon Agreement on file with VA and the
student is enrolled full-time, the form will ask you to enter your
institution's Yellow Ribbon contribution for the term. VA will match that
amount dollar-for-dollar up to the maximum in your agreement.

You can find more information about the Yellow Ribbon Program at
[/education/about-gi-bill-benefits/post-9-11/yellow-ribbon-program/](/education/about-gi-bill-benefits/post-9-11/yellow-ribbon-program/).

### Termination and reduction certifications

#### When should I submit a termination or reduction certification?

Submit a termination or reduction certification as soon as you know that a
student has withdrawn from all courses, stopped attending, was expelled, or
significantly reduced their enrollment. Prompt reporting helps VA avoid
overpaying the student, which would create a debt the student must repay.

#### What is a non-punitive grade?

A non-punitive grade is a grade that does not count toward academic credit —
such as a W (withdrawal), I (incomplete), or NC (no credit). Non-punitive
grades affect how VA calculates whether an overpayment occurred. The form
will ask whether any non-punitive grades were assigned for the term.

#### What are mitigating circumstances?

Mitigating circumstances are events beyond a student's control that caused
them to withdraw or reduce their enrollment — such as a serious illness, death
in the family, or military deployment. If mitigating circumstances apply, VA
may waive or reduce any overpayment debt. Describe the circumstances in the
field provided in the form.

## Where to get help

### Call us

**1-888-GI-BILL-1** (1-888-442-4551)
Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m. ET
TTY: 711

The VA Education Call Center can help you with questions about submitting
certifications, facility codes, processing status, and GI Bill payment issues.

### Chat with us online

[Start a chat with VA](#chat)
Available Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m. ET

### Contact your State Approving Agency (SAA)

Your SAA can help with questions about your institution's VA approval status,
facility code, program approvals, and Yellow Ribbon Agreement.
[Find your State Approving Agency](https://nasaa-vetseducation.com/contacts/)

### Contact your Regional Processing Office (RPO)

If you have a question about a specific certification that has already been
submitted, contact the VA Regional Processing Office that handles your state.
[Find your Regional Processing Office](https://www.benefits.va.gov/gibill/regional_processing_offices.asp)
