# Complete your VA Advance Directive online

**VA Form 10-0137 | OMB Control No. 2900-0556 | Expires: 04/30/2027**

An advance directive lets you tell VA your health care wishes now,
so others can follow them if you become unable to speak for yourself.
You can name a trusted person to make health care decisions for you
and describe the treatments you do or don't want.

This form is valid at all VA health care facilities. You don't need
a notary to use it within VA.

---

## Who can complete this form

You can complete this form if you:

- Are enrolled in VA health care, **and**
- Have a verified VA.gov account (Login.gov or ID.me at identity
  verification level LOA2/IAL2)

If you aren't enrolled in VA health care,
[find out how to apply](/health-care/apply/).

If you don't have a verified account,
[verify your identity on VA.gov](/resources/signing-in-to-vagov/).

---

## What this form lets you do

With VA Form 10-0137, you can:

- **Appoint a Health Care Agent** — a person you trust to make
  health care decisions for you if you can't make them yourself.
  You can also name an alternate in case your first choice is
  unavailable.
- **Document your living will preferences** — tell VA whether you
  want life-sustaining treatments in six different medical
  situations, such as if you are in a coma with little chance of
  recovery or if you have a terminal condition.
- **Record mental health care preferences** — describe medications
  that have worked for you or facilities you prefer or want to
  avoid.
- **Describe other care preferences** — include cultural, faith-based,
  or personal preferences about your care.
- **Choose how strictly to follow your preferences** — decide whether
  your preferences are a general guide or must be followed exactly.

---

## What to know before you start

### This form is valid inside VA — not automatically outside VA

This advance directive is valid at all VA health care facilities
**without notarization**. If you want it to be legally binding
outside VA (for example, at a community hospital), you may need
to have it notarized. State laws vary. Talk to a legal advisor
if you need it to work outside VA.

### You can save and come back

You don't have to finish in one sitting. Your progress is saved
automatically when you sign in. You can return any time to pick
up where you left off.

### Your information will be sent to your VA care team

After you submit, your advance directive goes directly into your
VA health record. Your VA care team will be notified. This
usually takes up to 3 business days.

### You can update or revoke this form at any time

A newer version of this form replaces the earlier one. You can
also tell a VA clinician in person that you want to revoke your
advance directive.

---

## What you'll need

Gather this information before you start. Your personal information
will be pre-filled from your VA profile, but you may want to confirm
it is current.

**For yourself:**
- Your name, date of birth, address, and phone number

**If you want to name a Health Care Agent:**
- Your Health Care Agent's full name, relationship to you,
  address, and phone number
- Your Alternate Health Care Agent's information (optional)

**For your two witnesses:**
- Each witness's full name and address

### Who can be a witness

Each witness must:
- Personally observe you completing and signing this form
- Be 18 years of age or older

Each witness **cannot** be:
- The person you named as your Health Care Agent
- Someone who is named as a beneficiary in your estate
- Someone who is financially responsible for your care
- Someone who signed the form on your behalf at your direction

**Estimated time to complete: 30 minutes**

## Prefer to use a paper form?

You can still
[download VA Form 10-0137 (PDF)](https://www.va.gov/find-forms/about-form-10-0137/)
if you prefer to complete it by hand. Print it, sign it with two
witnesses present, and bring or mail it to your VA facility.

- **Call us** at [1-800-698-2411](tel:18006982411)
  (TTY: [711](tel:711)), Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to
  8:00 p.m. ET

  complete your advance directive in person
