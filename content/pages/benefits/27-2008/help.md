# Help with the burial flag application

**VA Form 27-2008 — Application for United States Flag for Burial Purposes**

Find answers to common questions below. If you don't see your question, contact us using the information at the bottom of this page.

---

## Frequently asked questions

<va-accordion>

<va-accordion-item header="Who is eligible to receive a burial flag?">

A burial flag is provided for Veterans who were discharged or released from active military service under conditions other than dishonorable.

**Eligible Veterans include those who served in:**

- Active duty in the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard, Space Force, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), or U.S. Public Health Service
- Selected Reserve — if the Veteran completed at least one full enlistment, was discharged for a service-connected disability, or died while an active member
- National Guard — if the Veteran was called to federal active duty and completed that period of service

There are no wartime service requirements for Veterans who served after January 31, 1955. Veterans who served before that date must have served during a recognized wartime period, or have been discharged for a service-connected disability.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="Who can request a burial flag on behalf of the Veteran?">

The following people may apply, listed in order of VA's priority:

1. Surviving spouse
2. Children (including adopted children and stepchildren)
3. Parents (including adoptive parents)
4. Siblings
5. Other next of kin (nearest living relative when no one above is available)
6. A friend or associate — only if the Veteran left no next of kin and no next of kin is otherwise available

An authorized representative — such as a person with power of attorney — may also apply on behalf of an eligible family member.

Funeral directors may apply on behalf of a family using the paper VA Form 27-2008, available at any U.S. Post Office or VA regional office.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="Is the burial flag benefit available for Veterans with a dishonorable discharge?">

No. A burial flag is generally **not provided** for Veterans who received a dishonorable discharge.

If the Veteran's discharge was characterized as **Other Than Honorable** or **Bad Conduct**, VA will review the case individually before issuing a flag. These cases are not automatically approved or denied — a VA regional office will make a determination based on the specific circumstances.

If you are unsure about the Veteran's discharge status, you can request a copy of the DD-214 or equivalent discharge document from the [National Personnel Records Center (NPRC)](https://www.archives.gov/veterans/evetrecs).

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="I don't have the Veteran's DD-214. Can I still apply?">

You can still **start and submit** the application. We will note in your submission that the discharge document is not yet available.

However, we strongly recommend requesting the DD-214 as soon as possible. A missing discharge document is the most common reason for delays in burial flag delivery.

**How to request a DD-214:**

- **Online:** Submit a request through [eVetRecs at archives.gov](https://www.archives.gov/veterans/evetrecs) — this is the fastest option
- **By phone:** Call the National Personnel Records Center (NPRC) at **1-866-272-6272**, Monday–Friday, 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. CT
- **By mail:** Send a completed [SF-180 (Request Pertaining to Military Records)](https://www.archives.gov/veterans/military-service-records/standard-form-180.html) to the NPRC

**For Veterans who served before World War II:** Older discharge documents — such as the WD AGO 53-55 — are accepted in place of a DD-214.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="Does signing in with Login.gov or ID.me make a difference?">

Yes. When you sign in with a verified account before starting the form, we can:

- Pre-fill the Veteran's name, Social Security number, date of birth, branch of service, and dates of service from VA records — so you don't have to type it manually
- Reduce the risk of errors that can cause delays
- Save your in-progress application so you can return to it if you need a break

If you don't have a Login.gov or ID.me account, you can start the form without signing in. You'll need to enter all information yourself, and your progress won't be saved automatically.

[Learn how to create a Login.gov account](https://www.login.gov/help/get-started/create-your-account/)

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="How long does it take to receive the burial flag after I apply?">

For applications that are complete and the Veteran is confirmed as eligible, the flag is typically delivered to the funeral home or designated recipient within **1 to 2 business days**.

Cases that require additional review — such as those involving Reserve or National Guard service, character-of-discharge questions, or missing documentation — may take longer.

You will receive a confirmation email with a case reference number after you submit. Use this number if you need to follow up on the status of your application.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="How will the flag be delivered?">

You can choose how you want to receive the flag when you fill out the form:

- **Pickup at the funeral home** — the flag is delivered to the funeral home handling the arrangements
- **Mailed to a recipient address** — the flag is mailed directly to the person you designate
- **Other arrangement** — you can describe your specific circumstances in the form

Make sure the funeral home name, address, and phone number are accurate when you fill out the burial information section, so there are no delivery delays.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="What if the Veteran's service was with the National Guard or Reserve?">

National Guard and Reserve service has specific eligibility requirements that differ from active duty service.

**Selected Reserve members** are eligible if they:
- Completed their full initial obligation (one enlistment period), **or**
- Were discharged or released for a service-connected disability, **or**
- Died while an active member of the Selected Reserve

**National Guard members** are eligible if they were called to **federal active duty** (under Title 10, U.S. Code) and completed that period of service. State active duty does not qualify on its own.

When you fill out the form, select the appropriate branch and service type. The form will ask follow-up questions to determine eligibility based on your answers.

</va-accordion-item>

</va-accordion>
