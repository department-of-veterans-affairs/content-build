# Apply for a burial flag

**VA Form 27-2008 — Application for United States Flag for Burial Purposes**

The United States government provides a burial flag to honor a Veteran's military service. The flag is draped over the casket or placed with the urn during the funeral service, then given to the next of kin or another designated person to keep.

<va-alert
  status="info"
  headline="Time-sensitive benefit"
  visible>
  The burial flag must be available at or before the funeral service — usually within 24 to 72 hours of the Veteran's death. Apply as soon as possible after the Veteran's passing.
</va-alert>

---

## Can I apply for a burial flag?

You may use this form if you are requesting a burial flag for a **deceased Veteran** and you are one of the following:

- **Surviving spouse** — the person who was legally married to the Veteran at the time of death
- **Child** — including an adopted child or stepchild
- **Parent** — including an adoptive parent
- **Sibling** of the Veteran
- **Other next of kin** — the nearest living relative when no spouse, child, parent, or sibling survives
- **Authorized representative** — a person legally authorized to act on behalf of the family

<va-accordion>
<va-accordion-item header="Who qualifies as an eligible Veteran?">

The Veteran must have been discharged or released from active military service under conditions other than dishonorable. Eligible Veterans include those who served in:

- **Active duty** in the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard, Space Force, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), or U.S. Public Health Service
- **Selected Reserve** — if the Veteran completed at least one enlistment period, was discharged for a service-connected disability, or died while a member of the Selected Reserve
- **National Guard** — if the Veteran was called to active duty by federal order and completed the required active duty service

**Note about discharge status:** A burial flag is generally not provided for Veterans with a dishonorable discharge. If the character of discharge is Other Than Honorable or Bad Conduct, VA will review the case before issuing a flag.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="Are there wartime or peacetime service requirements?">

There are **no wartime service requirements** for the burial flag benefit. Veterans who served in peacetime are eligible if they otherwise meet the discharge requirements listed above.

**Veterans who served after January 31, 1955** are eligible regardless of whether they served during a recognized wartime period.

**Veterans who served before January 31, 1955** must have served in active duty during a recognized wartime period, or have been discharged or released from active duty for a disability incurred or aggravated in the line of duty.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="Can I apply if the Veteran's death occurred some time ago?">

You can apply for a burial flag if the funeral service has not yet taken place. The flag is intended to be presented at the funeral or memorial service.

If the date of death was more than 6 months ago, we will ask you to confirm the date before you continue. Contact your local VA regional office if you have questions about your specific situation.

</va-accordion-item>

<va-accordion-item header="Can a funeral director apply instead of the family?">

Funeral directors often help families request burial flags. This online form is currently designed for **next of kin and authorized representatives** to apply directly.

If a funeral director is handling the arrangements on behalf of the family, the family member may still complete this online form, or the funeral director may use the paper VA Form 27-2008 available at any U.S. Post Office or VA regional office.

</va-accordion-item>
</va-accordion>

## What documents and information will I need?

Gather these items before you start the form. Having them ready will help you finish in one session.

**About the Veteran:**
- Full legal name (as it appears on military records)
- Social Security number
- Date of birth
- Date of death
- VA file number (if known — optional)

**About the Veteran's military service:**
- Branch of service
- Dates of active duty service (entry date and discharge date)
- Character of discharge
- **DD Form 214** (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty) — or an equivalent discharge document

**About the funeral or memorial service:**
- Cemetery or place of burial name
- Funeral home name, address, and phone number
- Date of funeral or interment service (if scheduled)

**About yourself (the person filling out this form):**
- Your full name and relationship to the Veteran
- Your phone number and email address
- Your mailing address

<va-alert
  status="warning"
  headline="Don't have the Veteran's DD-214?"
  visible>

You can still start this application. The form will show you how to request military service records from the National Personnel Records Center (NPRC). You can submit your request at [eVetRecs (archives.gov)](https://www.archives.gov/veterans/evetrecs) or by calling **1-866-272-6272**.

Starting a records request now — before you complete this form — may help prevent delays in flag delivery.

</va-alert>

## How to apply

**Signing in gives you the fastest experience.** When you sign in with Login.gov or ID.me, we can:

- Pre-fill the Veteran's name, date of birth, branch of service, and dates of service from VA records — so you don't have to enter it manually
- Reduce the chance of errors that can delay your application
- Save your progress so you can return to the form if you need to step away

## How long will it take?

- **With sign-in and pre-fill:** About **5 to 10 minutes** to complete the form
- **Without sign-in:** About **10 to 15 minutes** to complete the form
- **If you need to locate a DD-214 first:** Plan additional time to request service records before completing the form

---

## What happens after I apply?

1. **We review your application.** VA reviews the information you submitted and verifies the Veteran's eligibility based on service records.
2. **The flag is authorized.** If eligible, VA authorizes the flag and sends a fulfillment request for physical delivery.
3. **The flag is delivered.** The flag is delivered to the funeral home or to the person you designate — typically within **1 to 2 business days** for complete applications.

You'll receive a confirmation email with a case reference number immediately after you submit. Save this number in case you need to follow up.
