# Request a VA home loan appraisal (VA Form 26-1805)

**VA-approved lenders:** Use this form to request a VA appraisal for a
Veteran borrower's home loan transaction.

**Veterans:** You cannot submit this form on your own. Your VA-approved
lender must submit it on your behalf. After your lender submits the
request, you can [check the status of your VA appraisal](#veteran-status).

## What this form is for

VA Form 26-1805 starts the VA appraisal process for a home loan
transaction. When you submit this form, VA assigns a VA-approved fee
appraiser to determine the reasonable value of the property. VA uses that
value to set the maximum loan amount VA will guarantee — called the
Notice of Value (NOV).

The NOV is valid for **180 days** from the effective date of the
appraisal.

---

## Before you start — check if an appraisal is required

<va-alert status="warning" aria-label="Warning: IRRRL transactions may not need an appraisal">
  <p><strong>IRRRLs may qualify for an appraisal waiver.</strong><br>
  If this transaction is an Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL,
  also called a Streamline Refinance), it may qualify for an appraisal
  waiver under VA Circular 26-18-13. Do not submit this form until you
  have confirmed with your VA Regional Loan Center that an appraisal is
  required for this specific transaction.</p>
  <p><a href="/resources/va-home-loan-regional-loan-centers/">
  Find your VA Regional Loan Center contact information</a></p>
</va-alert>

## Who can use this form

You can use this form if **all** of the following are true:

- You are a **VA-approved lender** with a valid 7-digit VA Lender ID
  number
- You **do not have access to the WebLGY lender portal**
- You are submitting an appraisal request for **a Veteran borrower's home
  loan transaction**
- The transaction is one of these loan types:
  - Purchase of a home
  - Cash-out refinance
  - IRRRL (Streamline Refinance) — only if you have confirmed an
    appraisal is required
  - New construction

**Note:** Mortgage brokers may submit this form on behalf of a
VA-approved sponsoring lender. The sponsoring lender's name and VA Lender
ID are required.

---

## What you'll need to complete this form

Gather this information before you start. The form will take approximately
**15 minutes** to complete.

**Lender information:**
- Your full legal lender institution name
- Your 7-digit VA Lender ID number (on your VA lender approval letter)
- Lender mailing address
- Point of contact name, phone number, and email address
- Lender type (bank, credit union, mortgage company, etc.)

**Veteran borrower information:**
- Veteran's full legal name
- Veteran's date of birth
- Veteran's Social Security number
- Veteran's Certificate of Eligibility (COE) number (if available)
- Whether the borrower is a surviving spouse of a Veteran

**Loan and transaction information:**
- Loan transaction type (purchase, refinance, new construction)
- Estimated purchase price or current estimated property value

**Property information:**
- Full property address, including ZIP code
- Property type (single-family, condo, manufactured home, multi-unit)
- Whether the property is existing construction or proposed/new
  construction
- Year built (for existing construction)
- Number of bedrooms and bathrooms
- Current occupancy status (owner-occupied, tenant-occupied, or vacant)
- Legal description (abbreviated)

**Additional information you may need:**

| Property type | Additional information required |
|---|---|
| Condominium or PUD | Condo project name, HOA name, and VA project approval ID (if known) |
| Manufactured home | Housing Identification Number (HIN), foundation type |
| New construction | Builder name, address, and license number; confirmation that plans and specs are available |

**Property access contact:**
- Name and phone number for the person who will let the appraiser into
  the property (listing agent, seller, current occupant, or property
  manager)
- Any lockbox or access instructions

**Prior VA appraisal (if applicable):**
- Prior VA case number and approximate appraisal date, if this property
  was previously appraised by VA

## How to apply

<va-process-list>
  <va-process-list-item header="Check if you need this form">
    Confirm you are a VA-approved lender without WebLGY access. WebLGY
    lenders should use the WebLGY portal. Confirm that an appraisal is
    required for this transaction (IRRRL transactions may qualify for a
    waiver).
  </va-process-list-item>
  <va-process-list-item header="Gather your information">
    Collect lender, Veteran borrower, property, and access contact
    information before you start. The form will time out after 60 minutes
    of inactivity, but your progress is saved automatically.
  </va-process-list-item>
  <va-process-list-item header="Complete and submit the form">
    Complete all required fields. The form checks for common errors before
    you submit, so you can fix problems right away. After you submit, you
    will receive a VA case number on screen. Save or print your
    confirmation.
  </va-process-list-item>
  <va-process-list-item header="VA assigns a fee appraiser">
    VA routes your request to the correct Regional Loan Center based on
    the property ZIP code. The RLC assigns a VA-approved fee appraiser,
    typically within 5 business days.
  </va-process-list-item>
  <va-process-list-item header="Appraiser schedules and completes the inspection">
    The fee appraiser contacts the property access person to schedule the
    inspection. The appraisal target is completed within 10 business days
    of assignment. Rural markets with fewer appraisers on the VA fee panel
    may take longer.
  </va-process-list-item>
  <va-process-list-item header="VA issues the Notice of Value (NOV)">
    VA reviews the appraisal and issues the NOV. The NOV establishes the
    maximum VA-guaranteed loan amount for the property. The Veteran has
    the right to receive a copy of the appraisal report.
  </va-process-list-item>
</va-process-list>

## After VA issues the Notice of Value

### What the NOV means for the loan

The NOV sets the maximum amount VA will guarantee for this property. The
NOV is valid for **180 days** from the effective date of the appraisal.
If the loan does not close within that window, a new appraisal request may
be required.

### The Veteran's right to a copy of the appraisal

The Veteran borrower has the legal right to receive a copy of the
appraisal report. Lenders are required to provide this copy to the Veteran
before loan closing.

### If the Veteran disagrees with the NOV

If the Veteran believes the value determination is incorrect, they may
request a Reconsideration of Value (ROV). The ROV process must be
initiated through the lender — Veterans cannot submit an ROV directly to
VA. For more information, see
[VA appraisal reconsideration of value](/housing-assistance/home-loans/reconsideration-of-value/).
