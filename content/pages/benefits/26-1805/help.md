# VA Form 26-1805 — Frequently asked questions

Get answers to common questions about VA Form 26-1805 (Request for
Determination of Reasonable Value) and the VA home loan appraisal process.

**On this page:**
- [For lenders: Submitting an appraisal request](#lender-questions)
- [For Veterans: Understanding your appraisal](#veteran-questions)
- [For everyone: The Notice of Value and next steps](#nov-questions)
- [Contact information and other resources](#contact)

<a id="lender-questions"></a>
## For lenders: Submitting an appraisal request

### Who can submit VA Form 26-1805?

Only **VA-approved lenders** with a valid 7-digit VA Lender ID number can
submit this form. Mortgage brokers may also submit this form, but only on
behalf of a sponsoring VA-approved lender. The sponsoring lender's name
and VA Lender ID are required.

Veterans cannot submit this form on their own. The form must come from a
VA-approved lender.

### I have WebLGY access. Should I use this form?

**No.** If you are a WebLGY-enabled lender, submit all appraisal requests
through the WebLGY lender portal. This VA.gov form is specifically for
lenders who do not have WebLGY access.

Submitting the same appraisal request through both VA.gov and WebLGY will
create duplicate cases. Duplicate cases cause delays and require staff
time to void one of the records.

[Go to the WebLGY lender portal](https://weblgy.vba.va.gov)

### Where do I find my VA Lender ID number?

Your 7-digit VA Lender ID number appears on your VA lender approval
letter from VA Loan Guaranty Service. If you cannot locate it, contact
your VA Regional Loan Center or call VA at
[1-800-827-1000](tel:+18008271000).

### Does my IRRRL transaction need an appraisal?

Not always. Many Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL) —
also called Streamline Refinance — transactions qualify for an appraisal
waiver under VA Circular 26-18-13. Before submitting this form for an
IRRRL, confirm with your VA Regional Loan Center whether an appraisal is
required.

If your IRRRL does require an appraisal, you may proceed with this form.
The form will ask you to confirm this before you can continue.

### Can I submit this form for a new construction property?

Yes, but new construction submissions receive additional handling. When
you indicate that the property is proposed or new construction, your
submission will be flagged for manual review by your VA Regional Loan
Center staff. This is because new construction requires a Construction
Compliance Inspection (CCI) workflow that cannot be fully automated.

You will need the builder's name, address, and license number, and you
must confirm that plans and specifications are available.

### Can I submit a VA appraisal request for a condominium?

Yes, but the condominium project must be **VA-approved** before you can
submit. The form checks VA's condo project approval database when you
enter the condo project name.

If the project is not VA-approved, you will see a hard stop explaining
that the project must complete the VA condo project approval process
before an appraisal can be requested.

[Learn about VA condo project approval
](/housing-assistance/home-loans/condo-project-approval/)

### What happens if the property is a manufactured home?

Manufactured homes require a specialized VA fee appraiser. When you
indicate that the property is a manufactured home, the form will ask for
the Housing Identification Number (HIN) and foundation type.

**Important:** VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPR) require that
manufactured homes be permanently affixed to a foundation. If the
manufactured home does not have a permanent affixed foundation, the
property does not meet VA MPR and the appraisal request cannot proceed.
The form will display a hard stop for non-permanent foundations.

### What is the ZIP code routing validation?

Your appraisal request is handled by the VA Regional Loan Center (RLC)
that serves the property's location. The form uses the property's ZIP
code to confirm routing to the correct RLC. If a ZIP code cannot be
matched to an RLC, the form will display an error before you can submit.

This validation prevents one of the most common rejection reasons on the
paper form — entering an incorrect property address that routes the
request to the wrong RLC.

### How do I get the submission confirmation with the VA case number?

After you submit the form, a confirmation page will display your **VA case
number** on screen. Save or print this page. The case number confirms
your submission was received and allows your team to track the appraisal
request. If you do not receive a case number, your submission was not
completed — check the form for error messages and try again.

### What if I submitted the form but made an error?

After submission, contact your VA Regional Loan Center immediately. Do
not submit a second form for the same transaction — this creates a
duplicate case. Provide your VA case number when you call.

[Find your Regional Loan Center contact information
](/resources/va-home-loan-regional-loan-centers/)

### How long does the form take to complete?

Most lenders complete the form in about **15 minutes** if they have all
the required information ready. The form saves your progress
automatically, so you can take a break and return to the same session
within 60 minutes. After 60 minutes of inactivity, the session may
time out.

## For Veterans: Understanding your appraisal

### Why can't I submit this form myself?

VA's appraisal request process requires a VA-approved lender to initiate
and submit the request. This is because the appraised value directly
affects the loan terms your lender is offering you, and VA requires the
lender to certify the accuracy of the information submitted. Contact
your lender to request that they submit the appraisal request.

### How do I know when my appraisal has been submitted?

Ask your lender. After your lender submits the request, they will receive
a VA case number confirming the submission. You can also sign in to
VA.gov to check your appraisal status directly — without calling anyone.

[Check your VA appraisal status
](/housing-assistance/home-loans/appraisal-status/)

### What are the stages of the VA appraisal process?

| Stage | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Request received** | VA has received the appraisal request from your lender. Staff are reviewing the request and will assign an appraiser. |
| **Appraiser assigned** | VA has assigned a VA-approved fee appraiser to your property. The appraiser will contact the access person to schedule the inspection. |
| **Inspection scheduled** | The appraiser has a confirmed date and time to visit the property. |
| **Inspection complete** | The appraiser has completed the property visit and is preparing the appraisal report. |
| **NOV issued** | VA has reviewed the appraisal and issued the Notice of Value. Your lender has received the NOV and will share it with you. |

### How long does the VA appraisal take?

The target is **10 business days** from when the appraiser is assigned
to when the appraisal inspection is complete, plus additional time for VA
to issue the Notice of Value. Most appraisals are completed within
**12 to 28 calendar days** from the date the lender submits the request.

**Rural markets** and areas with fewer VA-approved appraisers on the
fee panel may take longer. If the property is in a rural area, VA will
notify your lender about any expected extended timelines.

### Do I have the right to see the appraisal report?

**Yes.** Federal law requires your lender to give you a copy of the
appraisal report before your loan closes. If your lender has not
provided the report, ask for it.

### What if I disagree with the appraised value?

If you believe the Notice of Value does not reflect the fair market value
of the property, you have the right to request a **Reconsideration of
Value (ROV)**.

You cannot submit an ROV directly to VA — you must work with your lender
to initiate the request. Your lender submits the ROV to the VA Regional
Loan Center on your behalf.

To support an ROV, you and your lender can provide:
- Recent comparable sales (comps) that were not included in the original
  appraisal
- Factual errors you have identified in the original report

VA will review the ROV request and may adjust the value, confirm the
original value, or order a desk review.

[Learn more about the VA appraisal reconsideration of value process
](/housing-assistance/home-loans/reconsideration-of-value/)

### What does the Notice of Value mean for my home purchase?

The NOV sets the **maximum amount VA will guarantee** for the loan on
this property. The NOV is valid for **180 days** from the effective date
of the appraisal.

If the NOV value is lower than your purchase price, you have several
options:
- Request an ROV through your lender
- Negotiate a lower purchase price with the seller
- Pay the difference between the NOV value and the purchase price out of
  pocket (if permitted by VA and your lender)
- Walk away from the transaction (consult your purchase contract terms)

Your lender can help you understand your specific options.

### What happens if my loan doesn't close within 180 days of the appraisal?

If the loan does not close within 180 days of the effective appraisal
date, the NOV expires. Your lender may need to submit a new appraisal
request. Contact your lender if you are concerned about the timeline.

## For everyone: The Notice of Value and next steps

### What is the Notice of Value (NOV)?

The NOV is VA's official determination of the reasonable value of the
property being used as collateral for a VA-guaranteed home loan. It is
issued by the VA Regional Loan Center after a VA-approved fee appraiser
completes the inspection and submits the appraisal report.

The NOV:
- Sets the maximum VA-guaranteed loan amount for the property
- Confirms the property meets VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPR)
- Is valid for 180 days from the effective appraisal date

### What are VA Minimum Property Requirements?

VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPR) are safety, soundness, and
sanitation standards that all properties must meet to be eligible for
a VA-guaranteed loan. The fee appraiser checks for MPR compliance during
the inspection. If the property does not meet MPR, the appraiser notes
required repairs in the appraisal report.

Required repairs must typically be completed before VA will issue the
NOV. Your lender and the seller will need to agree on who pays for and
completes the required repairs.

### What if the property has required repairs?

If the appraiser identifies required repairs, the lender, buyer, and
seller will negotiate who completes the repairs. Once repairs are done,
the lender arranges a reinspection by the fee appraiser or another
VA-approved inspector. After VA confirms the repairs are complete, the
NOV can be issued.

- New construction and manufactured home appraisal questions
