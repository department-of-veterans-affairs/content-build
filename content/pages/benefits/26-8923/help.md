# Help with VA Form 26-8923 — IRRRL Worksheet

## Frequently asked questions

---

### About the worksheet

**What is VA Form 26-8923 and why do I need it?**

VA Form 26-8923 is the Interest Rate Reduction Refinancing Loan (IRRRL)
Worksheet. VA-approved lenders must complete this worksheet for every IRRRL
where they are requesting VA loan guaranty. The worksheet calculates the
maximum loan amount the lender may originate without disbursing cash to the
Veteran — which is prohibited on an IRRRL.

This digital tool replaces the paper PDF worksheet. It performs all 18
calculation steps automatically and generates a completed November 2024
version of the form.

---

**Who fills out this form — the lender or the Veteran?**

The lender fills out this form. An officer of the VA-approved lending
institution must review and certify the worksheet. Loan officers and
processors may prepare the calculation using this tool, but the printed PDF
must be signed in ink by an authorized officer before it goes into the
closing package.

Veterans do not complete this form. If you are a Veteran and want to
understand the maximum loan amount on your IRRRL, ask your lender to
share the completed worksheet with you.

---

**I've been using the paper form (PDF). Do I have to use this digital tool?**

For new IRRRL guaranty submissions, VA strongly encourages use of this
digital tool. It enforces the current November 2024 form version, calculates
all amounts automatically, and validates regulatory fee caps in real time.

If you use the paper PDF, make sure you are using the **November 2024
version** (OMB 2900-0386, exp. 11/30/2027). The December 2021 version is
superseded and should not be used.

---

**How long does it take to complete this worksheet?**

Most lenders complete the digital worksheet in 10 minutes or less, compared
to the 30-minute OMB estimate for the paper form. Have your servicer payoff
statement and fee information ready before you start to keep completion time
short.

---

### Entering loan data

**Where do I find the existing VA loan balance for Line 1?**

Get the current outstanding balance from a servicer payoff statement. Use a
current payoff statement — a stale or estimated balance is the most common
cause of errors on this worksheet.

If the Veteran is financing energy efficient improvements into the new loan,
add the cost of those improvements to the existing loan balance before you
enter Line 1.

---

**The tool won't let me enter an origination fee above 1%. Is that correct?**

Yes. Per 38 CFR § 36.4313, the origination fee for an IRRRL may not exceed
1% of the loan amount. The tool enforces this limit and will not let you
proceed with an origination fee above 1%.

If you believe a fee above 1% applies, review VA Lender's Handbook
(Pamphlet 26-7), Chapter 8, or contact your VA Regional Loan Center before
proceeding.

---

**The VA funding fee is pre-filled at 0.5%. When would I change this?**

The VA funding fee for an IRRRL is fixed at 0.5% per 38 CFR § 36.4312.
You should change this field to 0 **only** if the Veteran is exempt from
the funding fee.

Veterans may be exempt if they:
- Have a service-connected disability rated at 10% or more, **or**
- Are the surviving spouse of a Veteran who died in service or from a
  service-connected disability and are receiving Dependency and Indemnity
  Compensation (DIC).

**You must verify the Veteran's exemption status and include documentation
in the closing package.** Entering 0 without a valid exemption is a
regulatory violation. The digital tool will prompt you to certify the
exemption if you enter 0.

---

**What closing costs can I include in Line 8?**

Line 8 captures all other allowable closing costs and prepaids not already
entered in Lines 5, 6, or 7. These may include:

- VA appraisal fee (if obtained)
- Credit report fee
- Title search and title insurance
- Recording fees and transfer taxes
- Survey fee
- Hazard insurance premiums
- Prepaid items: property taxes, homeowners insurance

See VA Lender's Handbook (Pamphlet 26-7), Chapter 8, for the complete and
current list of allowable IRRRL closing costs. Do not include costs that
are not permitted by VA — those costs cannot be financed into the loan.

---

**What are discount points and when do they apply (Line 5)?**

Discount points are a fee the Veteran pays to reduce the interest rate on
the new loan. One discount point equals 1% of the loan amount. You enter
the percentage, and the tool calculates the dollar amount automatically.

Enter 0 if no discount points apply to this transaction.

Note: Discount points above 2% may require special justification per
38 CFR § 36.4313. The tool will display a warning if you enter more than
2%, but it will not block you from proceeding. You must verify that the
discount points amount complies with VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 8 before
closing.

---

### The Section III calculation

**Why does Section III recalculate values I already entered in Section II?**

Section III runs an iterative recalculation to find the true maximum loan
amount. This is necessary because the VA funding fee is calculated as a
percentage of the loan amount — but when the funding fee is financed into
the loan, it increases the loan amount, which increases the funding fee.
This creates a circular dependency.

The iterative calculation in Lines 10–18 resolves this circular dependency
mathematically. The digital tool performs all of this automatically. Line 18
is the final, correct maximum loan amount.

---

**Why must Line 18 always be rounded down?**

The IRRRL regulations prohibit cash being disbursed to the Veteran at
closing. Rounding up would increase the loan amount above the true maximum
and result in the Veteran receiving cash — which is prohibited.

The tool always rounds Line 18 down (toward zero). If the rounding
adjustment is $50 or more, the tool alerts you and confirms the recomputed
figure. If the rounding adjustment is less than $50, no recomputation is
required per the form's printed rule.

---

**Section III shows all dashes (—) instead of calculated values.
What's wrong?**

This means Section I or Section II has missing or invalid values. The
Section III calculation requires complete data from both earlier sections.
Return to the section with the missing information and complete all required
fields.

---

### After you submit

**After I submit the worksheet online, am I done?**

No. After you submit, you must:

1. **Download the generated PDF** from the confirmation page.
2. **Print the PDF** and have an authorized officer of your lending
   institution **sign it in ink** in the signature block.
3. **Include the signed worksheet** in your IRRRL closing package submitted
   through WebLGY or to your VA Regional Loan Center.
4. **Retain a copy** for your institution's records.

Checking the certification box in the digital tool does not substitute for
the required wet-ink signature on the printed form.

---

**What if the loan balance changes after I generate the PDF?**

If any input value changes before closing — for example, you receive a new
payoff statement with a different balance — you must return to this tool,
update the values, and generate a new PDF. Do not alter the generated PDF
manually. Use the confirmation page's "Start a new worksheet" button or
return to the [IRRRL Worksheet tool](/housing-assistance/home-loans/irrrl-worksheet-form-26-8923).

---

**What form version does this tool generate?**

This tool always generates the **November 2024 version** of VA Form 26-8923
(OMB Control No. 2900-0386, exp. 11/30/2027). It will not generate the
superseded December 2021 version. If a lender contacts you asking about the
older version, direct them to use this digital tool or the current PDF from
[va.gov/find-forms/about-form-26-8923](https://www.va.gov/find-forms/about-form-26-8923).

---

### Technical issues

**The tool says my origination fee exceeds 1%. I can't proceed.
What do I do?**

The tool enforces the 1% origination fee cap required by 38 CFR § 36.4313.
You cannot submit a worksheet with an origination fee above 1% of the loan
amount. Reduce the origination fee percentage to 1% or less to continue.

If you believe the fee is correct, review VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 8 or
contact your VA Regional Loan Center (see contact information below).

---
