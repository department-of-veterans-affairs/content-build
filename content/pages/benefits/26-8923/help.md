# Help with VA Form 26-8923 — IRRRL Worksheet

## Frequently asked questions

---

### General questions

#### What is VA Form 26-8923?

VA Form 26-8923 is the Interest Rate Reduction Refinancing Loan Worksheet.
VA-approved lenders complete this form to calculate the maximum loan amount
they can originate for an IRRRL while staying within VA regulatory limits.

The worksheet has three sections:
- **Section I** (Lines 1–3): Calculates the adjusted existing loan balance
- **Section II** (Lines 4–9): Calculates a preliminary loan amount by adding
  allowable fees
- **Section III** (Lines 10–18): Performs an iterative final calculation that
  accounts for the fact that the VA funding fee is based on the loan amount,
  which includes the funding fee itself

#### Who fills out this form?

An officer of a VA-approved lending institution fills out this form. Veterans
do not complete this worksheet themselves. If you are a Veteran with questions
about your IRRRL loan amount, ask your lender to explain the worksheet.

#### Why does the form need three sections? Why does it recalculate in Section III?

The VA funding fee is calculated as a percentage of the loan amount. But when
the funding fee is financed into the loan (rolled into the loan balance rather
than paid upfront), it increases the loan amount — which increases the funding
fee — which increases the loan amount again. Section III resolves this circular
calculation mathematically. The digital tool handles this automatically. You
don't need to perform the iterative steps by hand.

#### What version of VA Form 26-8923 should I use?

Always use the **November 2024 version**. Earlier versions — including the
December 2021 version — are superseded and may not reflect current regulatory
requirements. The digital tool always generates the November 2024 version. If
you use the paper form, check the version date on the face of the document.

### Using the digital tool

#### Do I need to create an account to use the digital worksheet?

Contact your VA Regional Loan Center for the most current guidance on access
requirements for this tool.

#### Can I save my work and come back to it?

This tool does not currently support saving a partially completed worksheet.
Gather all required information before you start. You should be able to
complete the worksheet in about 10 minutes with the information ready.

#### What if I make a mistake and need to change a value?

You can use the Edit links on the review page to go back and correct any
value. All calculated lines will update automatically when you make a change.
If you have already downloaded and printed a PDF and then discover an error,
return to the tool, complete the worksheet again with the correct values, and
generate a new PDF. **Do not alter a generated PDF manually.**

#### Why is the Line 18 amount different from what I calculated by hand?

The digital tool applies the round-down rule the form requires: the maximum
loan amount on Line 18 must always be **rounded down** (never up), to prevent
cash being disbursed to the Veteran. The tool also performs the full Section
III iterative recalculation automatically, which may produce a slightly
different result from a manual calculation if any intermediate rounding steps
were applied differently.

#### The tool shows an error saying my origination fee exceeds the cap. What does that mean?

The VA origination fee for an IRRRL cannot exceed 1% of the loan amount per
38 CFR § 36.4313. If you enter an origination fee percentage greater than 1%,
the tool will block you from continuing. Reduce the origination fee to 1% or
less. The tool displays the maximum allowable origination fee dollar amount to
help you check.

#### The tool shows an error saying my funding fee percentage is too high. What should I enter?

The VA funding fee for an IRRRL is **0.5%** per 38 CFR § 36.4312. This is the
only allowable rate for IRRRLs. The tool will not accept a percentage above
0.5%. If you believe a different rate applies to this loan, contact your VA
Regional Loan Center before proceeding.

#### What if the Veteran is exempt from the VA funding fee?

Enter **0** for the funding fee percentage (Line 7) if the Veteran is exempt.
Veterans are exempt from the VA funding fee if they:

- Receive VA compensation for a service-connected disability rated at 10% or more
- Are a surviving spouse of a Veteran who died in service or from a
  service-connected disability and are receiving Dependency and Indemnity
  Compensation (DIC)

When you enter 0 for the funding fee, the tool will ask you to confirm that
you have verified the Veteran's exemption status and that documentation is
included in the closing package. **You are responsible for verifying and
documenting exemption eligibility.** VA may independently verify this
information.

#### Does the tool verify the existing VA loan balance (Line 1) against VA records?

No. You must enter the existing VA loan balance from a current servicer payoff
statement. The tool cannot connect to servicer systems in real time. Using a
current payoff statement — not a stale balance from your origination
system — is critical for an accurate calculation.

#### Can I add energy efficient improvement costs to Line 1?

Yes. If energy efficient improvements are being financed into the new IRRRL,
add those costs to the existing loan balance on Line 1. Do not enter them
anywhere else on the form.

#### Why does the tool ask for a date and officer certification?

VA Form 26-8923 requires the signature and title of an officer of the lender.
The digital tool collects the completion date, officer name, and officer title
for inclusion in the generated PDF, and asks you to certify that the
worksheet is accurate and that the loan will not result in cash being disbursed
to the Veteran. **Checking the certification box does not substitute for the
required wet-ink signature on the printed form.** Print the PDF and have an
authorized officer sign it in ink before including it in the closing package.

#### What does "cash to the Veteran" mean and why is it prohibited?

An IRRRL must reduce the Veteran's interest rate and monthly payment (with
limited exceptions). The loan proceeds can only pay off the existing loan and
allowable closing costs — not provide cash back to the Veteran. If the maximum
loan amount calculation is incorrect and the loan amount exceeds allowable
limits, the excess becomes prohibited cash to the Veteran. The round-down rule
and fee caps enforced by this tool are specifically designed to prevent this.

### Technical issues

#### The tool is not loading or I'm getting an error message on the page.

Try these steps:

1. Refresh the page
2. Clear your browser cache and try again
3. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari are all supported)
4. Disable browser extensions that may interfere with the form

If the problem persists, contact the VA LGY Contact Center for technical
assistance (see contact information below).

#### The PDF I downloaded isn't opening.

Make sure you have a PDF reader installed (such as Adobe Acrobat Reader, which
is free). If the PDF appears blank or corrupted, return to the tool and
download it again. Do not submit a blank or corrupted PDF.

#### I submitted the worksheet but didn't download the PDF before closing the page.

Contact the VA LGY Contact Center with your confirmation number. Keep the
confirmation number shown on the final screen.

## Information for Veterans

If you are a Veteran whose lender is completing an IRRRL and you want to
understand how your maximum loan amount was calculated, you can ask your lender
to show you the completed VA Form 26-8923.

An IRRRL must:
- Refinance an existing VA-guaranteed loan
- Result in a lower interest rate (with limited exceptions for ARM-to-fixed
  conversions)
- Not result in cash being paid to you at closing

Your lender is required to certify that your loan amount does not exceed the
maximum calculated on this worksheet. If you have concerns about your loan
terms, contact a [VA-approved housing counselor](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/)
or reach out to the VA LGY Contact Center.
